Cryptography for AI and Trustworthy Ecosystems

A one-day forum for mechanisms, systems, attacks, measurements, definitions, and deployment perspectives on cryptographic trust across modern AI pipelines.

Call for presentations Important dates

Scope

Trust beyond a single model guarantee

Modern AI services are ecosystems. Data are collected, filtered, labeled, retrieved, forgotten, and reused. Models are trained, fine-tuned, routed, evaluated, and updated. Relays, APIs, agents, credentials, provenance layers, and auditing tools all become part of the security boundary.

CATE 2026 focuses on cryptographic methods that connect privacy, integrity, provenance, accountability, and auditability across that life cycle.

Topics

Questions for the AI ecosystem

Private data and memory

Private collection, retrieval, AI memory, data removal, and verifiable unlearning.

Honest services

Relay security, model/API accountability, benchmark integrity, and secure evaluation.

Verifiable computation

Proofs for model execution, private inference, approximate computation, and deployment limits.

Provenance and attribution

Watermarking, steganography, model/IP tracing, content provenance, and audit evidence.

Agent and toolchain security

Cryptographic controls for tools, credentials, policies, logs, and multi-party workflows.

Definitions and systems

Foundations, systematizations, attacks, measurements, and cross-community research directions.

Call

Presentations and posters

CATE 2026 invites presentation and poster abstracts on cryptographic methods for AI and trustworthy ecosystems. We welcome theory, protocols, systems, measurements, attacks, systematization, and research directions.

Submissions should be PDF abstracts of up to 3 pages, excluding references, written in English. There will be no formal proceedings, and submissions may describe published work, work in progress, or non-publishable but technically useful ideas.

  • Expected oral presentation length: 25-30 minutes, including questions.
  • At least one author of each accepted presentation or poster must register for the workshop.
  • Selection will consider suitability, technical interest, topical balance, and value to the Asiacrypt audience.

Format

One-day workshop

The proposed format is a full-day workshop with invited talks, community-contributed presentations, and a closing panel. The program is expected to include one or two invited long-form presentations and four to six contributed short presentations.

A poster session may be included if venue layout and scheduling permit, especially for early-stage work, system demonstrations, position papers, and discussion-oriented results.
