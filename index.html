About

Modern AI services are ecosystems. Data are collected, filtered, labeled, retrieved, forgotten, and reused; models are trained, fine-tuned, routed, evaluated, and updated; and relays, APIs, agents, credentials, provenance layers, and auditing tools all become part of the security boundary.

CATE 2026 aims to provide a focused forum for cryptographic work that addresses this broader AI ecosystem, beyond secure training and private inference alone.

Topics of Interest

  • Private data collection, retrieval, and AI memory
  • Privacy-preserving training and inference
  • Relay security, routing honesty, and API accountability
  • Backdoor prevention, detection, and auditability
  • Verifiable unlearning and model update evidence
  • Watermarking, steganography, provenance, and attribution
  • Secure evaluation and benchmark integrity
  • Agent, toolchain, credential, and policy security
  • Cryptographic definitions for AI accountability
  • Systems, attacks, measurements, and systematizations

Call for Presentations and Posters

CATE 2026 will invite short presentation and poster abstracts on cryptographic methods for AI and trustworthy ecosystems. Submissions may describe theory, protocols, systems, measurements, attacks, systematizations, research directions, published work, work in progress, or non-publishable but technically useful ideas.

Planned submission format: PDF abstracts of up to 3 pages, excluding references, written in English. Each abstract should include title, authors and affiliations, expected presenter, preferred format, a content summary, and a short explanation of relevance to CATE 2026.

Submission link and final instructions will be posted after the workshop is confirmed.

Planned Format

The proposed format is a full-day workshop with one or two invited long-form presentations, four to six contributed short presentations, a possible poster session, and a closing panel.

Contributed presentations will be selected from short abstracts. At least one author of each accepted oral or poster presentation must register for the workshop.
